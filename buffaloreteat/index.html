EXPERIENCE OUR HISTORY

The Buffalo River, just one of Arkansas' many rivers, is home to some of the most fun and unforgettable adventures. Flowing 135 miles through the Ozarks with many wildlife calling it their home. The Ozarks are protected by the National Parks Service. There are many different types of wildlife that live in the area as well. You can see deer, black bear, eagle, many different types of fish, other bird species, and the bountiful Arkansas Elk Herd. With so many activities to explore, there is something for everyone of all ages. Hike thought of some of the most breathtaking views, experiencing nature for yourself. Canoe down the river and see many of the wildlife that inhabit the Ozarks. Take a chance and leave the city behind!

1818

Osage Indians hunted the lands of the Buffalo River area. The Osage Indians give the land of the Buffalo over to the U.S Government.

1861-1862

Durning the Civil War, the Buffalo River Valley was split by the Union and the Confederate cause. The caves in the area were controlled by the Confederate army.

1890-1918

People started to acquired the land in the Buffalo River region. While it the land did provided plenty of food, it was hard for the families to make money. Many of them grew cotton and some other plants like goldenseal, genseng, sassafras and slippery elm to sell to dealers.

1930-1960

Durning the Depression, many families in the area were starving. The area's population started to see a fall. What helped these familes were when better roads were made.

1972

Became the first national river in the United States. One of the few free-flowing rivers and is about 150 miles. President Nixon siged the Public Law 92-237 which puts the river under protection of the National Park Service.

Now

The Buffalo River is still a beautiful spot today for families to go have a relaxing day in nature. Take a hike around the area, or canoe down the river for the day. A favorite spot for many families to just take a day away from the city and to just take in how amazing the area is. Lots of different wildlife also now inhabit the area as well.
